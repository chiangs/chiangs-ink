## The Problem

Operational and financial data in maritime
businesses rarely live in the same place —
and almost never tell the same story. Vessel
performance metrics sit in one system,
financial analysis in another, and the
people who need to act on both are left
reconciling reports manually, working from
data they're not sure they can trust.

For this maritime operator, the gap between
data and decision was costing time,
introducing error, and limiting the
analytical capability of the teams
responsible for performance and profitability.

The brief was to build from scratch — a
single internal B2B dashboard that unified
operational and financial analysis, made
the data trustworthy, and presented it at
the right time, in the right context, to
the right people.

<Challenge>
How do you design and build a dashboard
that earns trust — where the data is
reliable, the visualisations are genuinely
useful, and operational and financial
analysis tell a coherent story rather
than competing narratives?
</Challenge>

## The Process

I led the engagement across three disciplines
simultaneously — technology, frontend
engineering, and design — with a team of
a junior designer, a backend developer,
and a data scientist.

**Research & Design Leadership**
I led the junior designer through the full
design process from scratch — user research,
UX strategy, information architecture, and
UI design. This wasn't oversight from a
distance. It was active, hands-on leadership
through every phase, ensuring the design
decisions were grounded in how operational
and financial stakeholders actually think
about performance data — not how a
dashboard template assumes they do.

**Data Trustworthiness**
Before a single chart was designed, we had
to earn the right to visualise the data.
That meant working closely with the data
scientist to understand what the data
actually represented, where it was
unreliable, and what transformations and
validations were needed before it could
be presented as fact. Data that looks
precise but isn't trusted is worse than
no data at all.

**Visualisation Strategy**
The most deliberate work on this engagement
was the visualisation design. Operational
and financial data require different
cognitive modes — one is about pattern
recognition and anomaly detection, the
other about comparison and trend. We
researched, ideated, and prototyped
visualisation approaches that served
both modes without forcing users to
context-switch between two different
mental models.

The final visualisation system combined
four deliberate choices: bar charts for
temporal comparison and performance
trending; maps with near-real time updates
for spatial and operational awareness;
waffle charts for proportional data —
chosen over the default pie or donut
precisely because they allow users to
grasp overall proportion at a glance
while enabling drill-down into the
underlying detail; and typographic
hierarchy as a visualisation tool in
its own right — using scale, weight,
and contrast to direct attention to
the numbers that matter before a
user reads a single label.

Every choice was justified by how the
data needed to be understood, not by
what a charting library made easy.

**Engineering**
Built on Remix v2 with React, the
application was engineered for
performance and reliability — fast
load times on data-heavy views,
consistent rendering across browsers,
and an architecture clean enough
for the client to own and extend
after handoff.

<PullQuote>
The hardest problem wasn't building
the dashboard. It was making the
data worth looking at.
</PullQuote>

## The Outcome

The platform launched as an MVP in four
months — replacing manual reporting
processes and giving operational and
financial teams a single, trusted source
of truth for the first time.

The visualisation work produced interfaces
that went beyond standard charting —
purpose-designed to surface the specific
patterns and anomalies that matter in
maritime operations and financial
performance, rather than defaulting
to generic chart types that display
data without directing attention.

The junior designer shipped a production
UI having been taken through the full
design process from research to delivery
— a development outcome that reflects
how I approach team leadership: build
the capability of the people around you,
not just the product.

## The Infrastructure Debt

Maritime is one of the world's most data-rich
industries — and one of the least equipped to act
on it. Vessels generate continuous streams of
operational, performance, and services data.
Most of it sits in warehouses, siloed in legacy
systems, or never makes it off the ship at all.
The business value is there. The infrastructure
to surface it rarely is.

One of the world's largest maritime services
providers was no exception. Business-critical
vessel performance data and ship services metrics
were fragmented across legacy applications that
couldn't communicate — leaving operational
decision-makers without clarity, and the business
without the data foundation it needed to grow
and create new commercial opportunities.

The brief was unusually broad: start from scratch.
Design, build, and deliver an MVP the organisation
could own, extend, and eventually monetise —
including a path to exposing APIs as a future
revenue stream. No existing codebase. No existing
data strategy. A blank slate, a multifunctional
team, and five months.

<Challenge>
How do you build a business-critical platform
for global maritime operations — with offline
capability, a consolidated data strategy, and
a path to API monetisation — from zero, and
hand it over ready to scale in five months?
</Challenge>

## The Process

I led the engagement across four concurrent
workstreams simultaneously — product ownership,
scrum delivery, technical architecture, and
frontend engineering. Not sequentially. All at once.

**Product & Delivery**
We built the backlog from the ground up, ran scrum
ceremonies across a multifunctional team of designers,
backend developers, and data engineers, and maintained
the delivery rhythm that kept stakeholders aligned
across time zones and disciplines.

**Data Strategy**
The client's Snowflake data warehouse was
underutilised — data existed but wasn't structured
to deliver business value. We assessed the full
data landscape, mapped source-to-downstream
dependencies, and prescribed and executed a
strategy to extract clarity and quality from
what already existed. Critically, the work didn't
just serve the immediate platform — it structured
and positioned the business's data as a foundation
for future AI-powered experiences, moving from
raw operational data to an asset ready to be
reasoned over by intelligent systems.

**Technical Architecture**
The stack was chosen for longevity and ownership:
React Router v7 with a Backend-for-Frontend (BFF)
layer sitting in front of a .NET backend as the
single gateway to Snowflake. Offline capability
was built in from day one — not retrofitted —
because vessel operations don't wait for connectivity.

**Documentation & Compliance**
We produced extensive technical documentation that
directly supported the client's successful path
toward ISO27001 certification. Every architectural
decision, data flow, and system dependency was
mapped and documented to the standard required
for certification — a deliverable that outlasted
the engagement.

<PullQuote>
The platform didn't just replace two legacy systems.
It gave the business a data foundation it could

</PullQuote>

## The Outcome

The MVP launched to internal users across all 8 global
offices, covering over 300 vessels in live operation.
Two legacy applications were consolidated and retired.
The client took full ownership of the codebase with
the documentation, architecture, and backlog in place
to continue building and extending — including a
clear pathway to exposing and monetising APIs as
a future revenue stream.

The data strategy work repositioned Snowflake from
a passive warehouse into an active business asset —
with the data landscape fully mapped, quality
improvements prescribed, and the foundation laid
for three future opportunities: downstream data
products, API monetisation, and AI-powered
experiences built on clean, structured,
trustworthy data.

The engagement delivered three things most projects
deliver one of: a working product, a data strategy,
and an organisation ready to own and scale both.
